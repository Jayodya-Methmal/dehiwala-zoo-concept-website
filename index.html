Dehiwala Zoological Garden

The National Zoological Gardens of Sri Lanka, established in 1936, is a zoological facility in Dehiwala, Sri Lanka. Its vast expanses are home to a wide range of animals and birds. The zoo not only showcases animals, but it also focuses on animal conservation, welfare, and education.

The National Zoological Gardens, also known as the Dehiwala Zoo, is a 30 acre well-landscaped zoo with a diverse assortment of birds, reptiles, fish, and mammals. One of Asia's oldest zoos is the Dehiwala Zoo.

Sri Lankan Wildlife Experience

Despite its tiny size, Sri Lanka has one of the world's highest rates of biological endemism, both in plants and animals, and is one of the world's top five biodiversity hotspots. Asian elephants, sloth bears, leopards, sambar, and wild buffaloes are among the ninety-one species of animals present in Sri Lanka that attract the most interest from wildlife enthusiasts.

The Dehiwala National Zoo, which has a remarkable collection of exotic and indigenous fauna, is one of the remarkable Zoos in Asia.
