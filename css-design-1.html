Types of Animals

Dolphins

There are 36 species of dolphins in the Delphinidae family. Because certain members of the dolphin family share the term whale in their common name, it might be confusing at times. In fact, the killer whale is the largest dolphin! Dolphins come in a variety of colors, ranging from white, pearl, and pink to deeper tones of brown, gray, blue, and black, depending on the species.

Dolphins have smooth skin, flippers, and a dorsal fin. They have a long, slender snout with about 100 teeth, and a streamlined body. The single blowhole on top of their head has a flap that opens to reveal a pair of nostrils, which dolphins use for breathing when they surface..

Habitat and Diet

To eat, dolphins cooperate. They have the ability to herd schools of fish together for group feeding. Some even herd the fish with their clicking sounds. Bottlenose dolphins surge up onto mud banks in the hopes of catching stranded fish. Fish, octopus, squid, and shrimp are the most common foods for dolphins. Seals, smaller whales, penguins, birds, and even small walruses are all part of the killer whale's diet.

Dolphins live and play in pods ranging in size from five to hundreds of individuals. Some people can swim and roll in formation in the same way that synchronized swimmers do. Dolphins also aid each other when one of them is sick, injured, or giving birth. They alternate pulling the injured or juvenile dolphin to the surface to let it to breathe. Dolphin pods will attack an intruder as a group, and they can even kill a huge shark by ramming it!
