Animals

Cheetah

Fastest mammal on land, the cheetah can reach speeds of 60 or perhaps even 70 miles (97 or 113 kilometers) an hour over short distances. It usually chases its prey at only about half that speed, however. After a chase, a cheetah needs half an hour to catch its breath before it can eat.

These cats can turn quickly and abruptly in pursuit of prey and are dexterous at high speeds. From their long, slender limbs and firm foot pads to the flexible spine that gives them their lengthy stride, cheetahs' physique are especially built to help them reach top speeds. Cheetahs are known for their tawny coats covered in black dots, each placed in a distinctive pattern to aid in the identification of the animals. .

LIFE SPAN


about 12 years

Length


Adult: 67 to 94 cm

Weight


21 to 72 kg

Habitat and Diet

Cheetahs are carnivores who hunt during the day, usually around dawn or dusk when it's cooler. Antelope, ground-dwelling birds, rabbits, porcupines, and ostrich flesh make up their diet. Because chasing after a cheetah requires a lot of energy, there is usually a recovery period after a chase. Cheetahs must eat swiftly to escape other carnivores such as lions, vultures, and hyenas stealing their prey.

Female cheetahs are normally solitary and hunt alone, with the exception of occasional mother-daughter pairs. Females are responsible for raising their cubs on their own. The mother leaves at 18 months, and the cubs form a sibling group that will stay together for another 6 months. The female cubs go, and the males form a "coalition" that stays together for the rest of their lives.
